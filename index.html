Legal Guide

TM Users' Guide to the SIM Card Registration Act

1
⚖️

The SIM Card Registration Act changed how mobile users in the Philippines use and manage their numbers, especially for prepaid subscribers like TM users. This law was introduced to reduce text scams, fraud, and anonymous misuse of mobile numbers by making sure every SIM is linked to a verified identity. For TM users, understanding the basic rules of this Act helps you stay compliant, protect your number, and avoid sudden deactivation or service loss.

2
📋

Under the SIM Card Registration Act, all new SIM cards are sold in a deactivated state and will only be activated after successful registration with the correct information. Existing users were given a specific registration period to submit their details; missing the deadline could lead to the SIM being blocked until properly registered again. For TM subscribers, this means your number must be tied to your real identity using valid documents, not nicknames or placeholder information. Treat your registered details the same way you treat your banking or government records.

3
🆔

When you register your TM SIM under this law, you need to provide key personal information. This usually includes your full name, date of birth, sex, and current address, all of which must match what appears on your valid ID. Accepted IDs can be a national ID, passport, driver's license, or other government-issued identification with a clear photo and signature. During the process, you also confirm your mobile number and, in some systems, the SIM or card serial number, to make sure the record is linked to the correct physical card.

4
👥

The Act also sets rules for special cases. For minors, the SIM must be registered under the name of a parent or legal guardian, with that adult taking responsibility for the number's use. For corporate or organizational accounts, registration is done under the legal entity, usually managed by an authorized representative. TM users in remote areas are not left behind; the law requires that registration support be made available even where internet access is limited, often through assisted registration booths or partner locations.

5
🔒

Another important aspect of the SIM Card Registration Act is how it affects your privacy and data security. Telecom companies are required to maintain a secure database of subscriber information and are not allowed to use it for purposes outside the scope of the law, except in cases defined by regulation or court orders. This means your registration data should be handled with strict safeguards, and any access to it must follow clear legal processes. As a TM user, you still have the right to ask questions about how your information is stored and protected.

6
⚠️

Finally, the law has direct consequences if you do not comply or if your information is false. A SIM that is not registered within the allowed period can be deactivated, leading to loss of access to calls, texts, data, and one-time passwords that many online services send to your number. Submitting fake details or using someone else's identity can also lead to penalties under the law. For prepaid subscribers, it is also wise to keep an eye on reminders from your provider about registration deadlines and Prepaid SIM Expiry, because allowing a registered SIM to expire unused for a long time can still result in disconnection according to the provider's own policies. By understanding these core points of the SIM Card Registration Act, TM users can register properly, keep their information accurate, and maintain a secure, reliable mobile connection in line with Philippine rules.
